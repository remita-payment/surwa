March 05, 2025

Adult Literacy Program

Yobe State Agency for Mass Education (SAME)

In a bid to strengthen partnership and collaboration, a team from Surwa Community and Education Development Initiative (SCEDI) paid an advocacy visit to the Yobe State Agency for Mass Education (SAME) on Wednesday, 5th March 2025. The SCEDI team, led by the Executive Director, was accompanied by senior officials, including the Education Officer, Data Management Officer, WASH Officer, and Finance and Admin Officer.

The primary objective of the visit was to brief the Agency on SCEDI's ongoing adult literacy program, funded and implemented by the organization. The program, currently in its sixth month, provides free education to adult men and women, with the ultimate goal of mainstreaming them into formal junior and secondary schools after a nine-month period.

During the visit, the SCEDI team engaged in fruitful discussions with the Executive Director of Yobe SAME, exploring avenues for collaboration and technical support to ensure the successful implementation of the adult literacy program. The meeting also provided an opportunity for SCEDI to seek guidance and expertise from the Agency in monitoring and evaluating the program.

The advocacy visit underscores SCEDI's commitment to promoting education and community development in Yobe State. By working together, SCEDI and Yobe SAME aim to make a positive impact on the lives of adult learners and contribute to the overall development of the state.

September, 2024

Visitation to Adult Literacy Center

Damaturu

The Surwa Community and Education Development Initiative (SCEDI) has taken a significant step towards promoting adult literacy in Damaturu with the launch of its self-funded "Partnership for Accelerated Adult Literacy Program" in September 2024. The program aims to equip adults with essential literacy and numeracy skills, preparing them for future formal education opportunities.

The program, scheduled to run until 2027, has a nine-month first phase concluding in March 2025. SCEDI's Executive Director, accompanied by organization officers, recently visited adult literacy centers in Damaturu to monitor progress and ensure the initiative's effectiveness.

SCEDI's dedication to education and community development is evident in this self-funded initiative. The organization's efforts demonstrate the importance of collaborative partnerships in promoting literacy and education.

June 05, 2024

Nigerian Environmental Society (Yobe State Chapter)

Town Square, Damaturu

Surwa Community and Education Development Initiative (SCEDI) demonstrated its commitment to environmental sustainability by participating in the World Environment Day 2024 event organized by the Nigerian Environmental Society (Yobe State Chapter). The event was held under the theme: Land Restoration, Desertification, and Drought Resilience.

Through its participation, SCEDI aimed to inspire and encourage individuals, organizations, and policymakers to take proactive steps towards sustainable land management and the preservation of natural resources.

As an organization dedicated to community development, SCEDI remains steadfast in its efforts to raise awareness, promote education, and drive positive change in environmental practices.

By actively engaging local communities, promoting environmental education, and implementing sustainable practices, we can make a significant difference in creating a more resilient and sustainable future.

May 28, 2024

United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA)

Government Day Junior Secondary School Bindigari, Damaturu

Surwa Community and Education Development Initiative (SCEDI), in partnership with the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA), organized a one-day sensitization session for adolescent girls on menstrual hygiene and gender-based violence (GBV) in schools.

Menstrual Hygiene Day is celebrated annually on the 28th of May to raise awareness about menstrual health and hygiene among girls and women.

The sensitization session, held at Government Day Junior Secondary School Bindigari in Damaturu, aimed to equip adolescent girls with effective knowledge and strategies for managing menstruation and addressing GBV in school settings.

In line with this year’s theme, #PeriodFriendlyWorld, SCEDI and UNFPA distributed sanitary pads to 50 adolescent girls in the school to ensure access to essential menstrual hygiene products and uphold their dignity.

Three presentations were delivered during the event on the topics of Prevention of Sexual Exploitation and Abuse (PSEA), Gender-Based Violence (GBV), and Menstrual Hygiene Management. The presentations were facilitated by Hajara Suleiman (UNFPA), Musa Bala Musa (UNFPA), and Dr. Hauwa Amshi, respectively.

Together, we can create a #PeriodFriendlyWorld.
